The Problem

My "Smart" Pump Isn't Very Smart

I wear an insulin pump and a continuous glucose monitor.

But it really only knows two things:

1
My current glucose
2
Carbs I'm about to eat

The Gap

What My Pump Doesn't Know

  • I ran 2 miles this morning — I'm more sensitive for the morning
  • I slept 4 hours last night — My glucose will run higher
  • I'm eating pizza, not salad — Fat causes delayed spike 4 hours later
  • I'm getting sick — My needs are about to double

I adjust for all of this manually. Every day.

How It Works

Step 2: Effective Parameters

Your doctor gives you base settings:

Insulin Sensitivity Factor
1 unit drops 50 mg/dL
Insulin to Carb Ratio
1 unit covers 10g carbs

But these change with context. Dossi calculates your effective parameters right now.

Key Innovation

Bayesian Learning

Dossi doesn't use hardcoded rules like exercise = +20%

It learns your patterns from actual glucose outcomes across all factors.

Continuously learning from:

1.Meals, exercise, sleep, stress, time of day
2.Observe outcomes vs predictions
3.Build personalized modifiers for each factor

How It Learns

The Learning Loop

After every meal, Dossi observes what actually happened:

1.
Predict — "Given this context, glucose should land at X"
2.
Observe — "Actual glucose was Y" (higher or lower?)
3.
Attribute — "Which factors were active?"
4.
Update — "Adjust belief about how that factor affects ME"

Safety First

4 Layers of Validation

Insulin errors can be fatal. Every recommendation passes 4 checks:

1.
Hard Limits — Never dose below user-specified amount
2.
Predictive — Hypo risk at 15/30/60/120 min?
3.
Anomaly — Is this sensor data trustworthy?
4.
Learning — Have recent outcomes been poor?
